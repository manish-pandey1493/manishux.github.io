VisaMate

Scroll to Final Prototype

UX Design • User Research • Prototyping

Providing a comprehensive, step-by-step approach to assist immigrants in navigating the visa journey process.

Project Timeline

  • January 2022
  • 8 Weeks

My Role

  • Solo UX Designer
  • User Research

Tools Used

  • Figma
  • Google Forms
  • Figjam
  • Protopie

Problem

Immigrants struggle with the U.S visa application process.

John, a fiancé of a U.S citizen who is applying for a K-1 visa, expresses frustration with the visa processing journey due to the lack of a platform where he can access filled form samples and his upcoming steps.

How might we make the visa processing journey less frustrating?

Sneak Peek Into Solution

Step-By-Step Guidance

  • Sequential approach to assisting immigrants in determining their next steps.
  • Detailed explanation of each step in the process with respective form samples.
  • Concise and explicit guidance on verifying the necessary documents at each specific step.

Form Samples

  • A search function to access relevant form samples for every required document.
  • Instructions provided in sections for users to fill out forms accurately.
  • A comprehensive checklist to facilitate a smooth and error-free form completion process.

Documents Checklist

  • Checklists for each document to ensure users have all the required documents when submitting their application.
  • A convenient checkbox feature that serves as a reminder, eliminating the need for users to manually note down each required document.
  • Required documents for both the sponsor and the applicant are segregated for easy organization and retrieval.

Translation and Services

  • In-app translation services to enable users to access all the services within the application.
  • Displaying lawyers or professionals who are located in the user's chosen area.
  • Booking a lawyer through the app for translation and document verification services.

Let's dive into how I did it - Approach

Market Research

According to the U.S. Department of State, in fiscal year 2021 (which runs from October 2020 to September 2021), there were approximately 462,000 immigrant visa applications submitted worldwide. This includes applications submitted by family members of U.S. citizens and permanent residents, as well as those submitted by individuals seeking employment-based visas, diversity visas, and other categories.

So what about this numbers?....Hmmm, How about finding business opportunities and solving user problem at the same time?

What are the possibilities?

This all the numbers of immigrants are using one or other ways to help themselves in their visa journey. How about we analyze the similar product that is trying to solve the user problem. I feel glad that I got to connect with so many immigrants who were in their visa journey process and got to know the app named "Lawfully" which was somewhat trying to solve the same problem. "Really appreciate their effort".

Some of the features that were not available on the app but might be crucial in the visa journey process :

  1. Step-By-Step guidance [What's next step?]
  2. Documents checklist [What document to submit on each step?]
  3. Sample forms for reference
  4. Translation and notary services around user

Who are our potential users?

Conducting a thorough market research and analyzing the demographics of immigrants who actively participate in social media groups have enabled me to identify the target audience beforehand, which I believe would greatly benefit the design process.

Conducting User Research

During the project, I started with the qualitative user research for getting insights from the potential users. The qualitative user research method I used during the empathize phase was User interview.

Research Methods

  • User Interviews
  • Thematic Analysis
  • Discourse Analysis

Rationale

  • I had the opportunity to connect with many individuals who were waiting for their visas, and conducting actual interviews with them would be meaningful and valuable.
  • Thematic analysis would give me an opportunity to analyze the data that I collect from the user interviews and understand the recurring themes or patterns.
  • Through the utilization of Discourse analysis, I gained a deeper comprehension of the users' language tone and communication patterns during interviews, especially in relation to the specific problem I sought to solve.

The actual process :

  1. Setting Goal

    2. I initiated the research with the aim of identifying the pain points experienced by users during their immigration visa processing. In addition to this, I was looking for the platform that had the highest level of user activity and preference. To prioritize the investigation of the most severe issues, I ranked the questions according to their level of severity.

  2. Recruiting relevant participants

    3. Recruiting relevant participants is more important as it can provide more relevant insights from the actual potential users that would use the product. Trying to get participants for the interview was like trying to convince a cat to take a bath - a real challenge!. A group of 6 participants were selected for the interview.

    "Key Takeaways: Always appreciate the time and effort that users invest in interviews. Additionally, strive to ensure participants don't leave empty-handed. For instance, after my interviews, I provided refreshments and snacks, which was well-received and appreciated."

  3. Conducting Interviews

    4. During the interviews, I observed user behaviours and recorded the conversation with their consent to find out more insights later during the decision-making. Some of the questions that were asked during the interviews were:

    Questions Rationale
    1. How do you check your visa case status? To understand the primary platform utilized by the users.
    2. Are you waiting for your immigration visa? If yes, how long has it been that you applied for the visa? To empathize the challenges that users are presently encountering due to longer waiting periods.
    3. Are there any existing apps or tools that you currently use to track your visa status? To gain a better understanding of the features offered by similar app or identify areas where it may be lacking
    4. What additional features would you like to see in an app that helps track immigration journey? To find out if users require specific features that could assist them in their visa application process.
  4. Analyzing Insights

    5. After conducting interviews, I came up with a lot of insights in the form of different narration and user behavior. I used these method to analyze the findings and some of them are listed below:

    • Thematic Analysis

      • During the analysis, every feature discussion and user sayings were divided into “Positive experience” and “Negative experience” to analyze each insights. I identified and isolated the recurring frustrations expressed by the users, segregating them from other insights.

    • Discourse Analysis

      • Every sentence from the participants were analyzed including the tone, style and underlying attitudes and beliefs. I analyzed the tone of the participants' frustration regarding various pain points and explored their backgrounds.

  5. Creating Persona

    6. After the collection of insights from the interview, I created user personas that could represent the target audience and provide a clear understanding of their goals, motiavations and behaviors.

  6. Findings

    7. From the above analysis, I came to find that 80% of the users were using the mobile devices to track their case.Also, some recurring frustrations from the users that were actually using the similar products was found and they were:

    Indication of Step-By-Step process

    Applicants had issues finding out the next step in their visa processing journey.

    Form Samples and Instructions

    Applicants had hard time figuring out some of the information that needed to be filled out in their forms related to immigration.

    Real-time Case Tracking

    They had to open their app in order to find out the updates about the case everytime they wanted to know their case status.

Design decisions based on the research

After the research, I used the insights to make the following design-decisions.

Focusing on mobile platform

After analyzing the research findings, it became evident that the optimal platform for our solution is a mobile device, which led to the decision to develop a dedicated mobile app.

Indication of Step by Step Process

Applicants should be aware of the steps that are going to occur during their visa application process.

Filing Form Instruction or Samples

Providing a form sample with all fields completed and a set of instructions to aid applicants in understanding the purpose of each section.

Clean UI (Uncluttered UI)

Ensuring that the user experience is accessible and user-friendly for all applicants, a clean and functional user interface can be implemented, regardless of their level of familiarity with the system.

Crafting Design Ideas

Based on the findings of the research, I have developed a list of design ideas and features. These insights encompassed a range of design considerations, including developing a streamlined user interface, implementing a step-by-step visa application process, enabling users to track their case status, providing access to a list of required documents, and offering assistance with notary and translation services through access to a network of lawyers.

Feature-1

User friendly interface :

The app should be designed with a user-friendly interface that is easy to navigate, with clear instructions and guidance throughout the visa application process.

Feature-2

Clear step-by-step guidance :

The app should be able to provide step-by-step guidance while filing out the visa application, including detailed instructions on how to complete each section of the application form.

Feature-3

Documents checklist :

The app should include a checklist of required documents in every step of the visa application process. In addition to this, instructions on how to obtain each of them should also be included.

Feature-4

Translation service with verified lawyer :

The app should include a translation service in collaboration with verified lawyer from specific region that helps user to translate their documents into English or the language required for the visa application.

Designing Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Once I have brainstormed and generated multiple ideas, I analyze design ideas and try to create a low-fi version wireframe to get the general app flow.

Creating App Flow

From low-fi wireframe sketching, I got to know what the general app flow should look like and started working on making it as human centered as possible.

How might we make the onboarding as seamless as possible?

Onboarding Ideas

As creating a case when onboarding to the app is the most crucial part and as a designer I tried to create the process as seamless as possible. During which I came up with two ways for creating a case when onboarding users.

Now it was time to find the optimal solution that is backed up by some actual user testing. Thus, I conducted A/B testing between these two variants of screen. I also hypothesized that the inclusion of radio-button and dropdown list (Variant-1) for the immigrant and non-immigrant visa category would be easier for user to select.

Conducting A/B Testing

During the user testing, my primary focus was on the task completion rate and time spent on page metrics for participants. I prioritized these metrics as they provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of the design. Specifically, a longer time spent by a participant in completing the task indicates that the design may be less effective. Participants were also requested to Think-out-loud during the testing as it could allow a lot of insights about what they are thinking at a particular task.

Analyzing Insights

The data collected during the interviews was organized in a chart format to facilitate the comparison of the effectiveness of Variant 1 versus Variant 2.

So you might be thinking why I would consider time-taken by a participant as decision making factor?.... The answer is, it is the time-taken by a participant to succesfully select their own visa category.

Implementing the winning variant

Creating an Initial Prototype

Prior to advancing to the final prototype, an initial prototype was developed in order to validate the design concepts through the utilization of usability testing.

Conducting Usability Testing

During the usability testing, participants were requested to complete a series of tasks, and the following metrics were employed to assess their performance: Task completion time, Success Rate, Error Rate, and User Engagement.

How about the prompt tasks that were given and the rationale behind them?

Prompt Task Rationale
Can you please walk me through the process of onboarding and signing up for the app? To evaluate whether all users are capable of successfully completing the onboarding process.
Please select the particular visa category that is associated to your case to enable tracking. To understand if the user is able to choose the visa category and enter their receipt number easily.
Please book a lawyer for translating your documents. To check if users find the booking feature easy to use.
Please check the upcoming steps in the visa application process. To understand if the feature is usable by every age group of visa applicants.

Insights

Following the usability testing, numerous insightful findings were uncovered and among which the critical ones were selected and I started iterating them.

"Despite wearing my glasses, I am unable to view the lawyer's profile description and the button to access it, as they appear excessively small to me."

- IR5 Applicant (Parents of U.S citizen)

"When exploring the community tab, I repeatedly clicked on the 'plus' button , only to become frustrated upon realizing that the button remained visible and did not disappear."

- CR1 Applicant (Spouse of U.S citizen)

How did I iterate?

Solving 1st issue -

Upon receiving the insights from the usability testing, it was observed that the Error rate for selecting the lawyer's profile was high, possibly due to the relatively small size of the button's touch area. Additionally, it was noted that the text appeared somewhat challenging to read, potentially due to the greyish text color. Based on this feedback, an iterative process was carried out to address this issue.

Solving 2nd issue -

The placement of the "Creating Post" feature as a float button resulted in some confusion among users, particularly those aged between 40-55 years. As a solution, a more distinct "Create Post" button was introduced at the top of the screen to improve clarity and ease-of-use.

Final Prototype

Following the design iteration, a final prototype was developed to closely resemble the product.

Final Usability Testing and Success Metrics

Following the design iteration and creation of the final prototype, a usability testing was conducted to validate the design changes

The results showed that 90% of the users successfully completed the given prompt tasks, indicating a positive outcome.
