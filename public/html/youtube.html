YouTube Co-Watch

Scroll to Final Prototype

UX Design • User Research • Prototyping

Enabling users to co-watch videos seamlessly using the existing YouTube platform.

Project Timeline

  • November 2022
  • 4 Weeks

My Role

  • Solo UX Designer
  • User Research

Tools Used

  • Figma
  • Google Forms
  • Figjam
  • Protopie

Problem

Distance often creates a challenge for people wanting to watch videos together with friends and family who are far away from them.

John, a new immigrant, feels alone being far away from his home. He simply wants to spend quality time with his family by watching movies on YouTube. Unfortunately, there's no intuitive way to co-watch videos together.

How might we leverage the social aspect of connecting people via YouTube's watching sessions?

Before diving into solution

Before delving directly into the solution, I had curiosity regarding two crucial aspects:
i) Alignment with YouTube's mission.
ii) Whether the feature holds a mutually beneficial impact.

Understanding the needs of both sides, an introduction of co-watch feature would make a win-win situation.

Users will gain the opportunity to enjoy their favorite videos alongside their loved ones, while YouTube stands to benefit from an increased user base and higher watch time on its platform.

Research and Findings

Before moving on to the ideating different ideas, I asked 5 Youtube users how they managed to watch the videos together and some of the findings were bizzare.
One user mentioned, "We used to watch movies by sharing screens while making calls on Facebook."

Now, let's envision this feature impacting YouTube's 2.9 billion active users, while also contributing positively to the platform's watch time.

Understanding the user flow

Before implementing the feature, let's analyze the current user flow of the youtube and try aligning the co-watch feature in it.

Brainstorming

Finding right path for user to create a room to co-watch their favorite ideas.

Deciding on the userflow

After analyzing various ideas, the optimal location for the co-watch feature could be the existing create button on the navigation bar.

Why did I choose to position the co-watch button within the create button?

  • Minimal Cognitive Load

    • Placing the co-watch option within the create button reduces cognitive load for users. They don't need to search for a separate button or menu, streamlining their interaction with the platform.

  • Consistency

    • Placing the co-watch option alongside other creation-related actions maintains a consistent design pattern which enhances the overall user experience and avoids cluttering the interface with multiple buttons.

  • Space Utilization

    • The create button likely occupies a significant amount of navigation bar as all other buttons. Utilizing this space efficiently by incorporating the co-watch option inside the already existing create button makes efficient use of UI resources.

Iterating Designs

After exploring various design concepts and engaging in a critique session with my designer colleague, the high-fidelity designs were created.

Impact

Increased User Base

The availability of the co-watch feature would attract many new users to the platform and contribute to YouTube's mission of enabling users to share their content with the world.

Increase in watch time

Watching long movies and videos on YouTube with friends and family would significantly increase the platform's watch time.

Strengthening the social aspect of the platform

Given YouTube's status as a social platform, the co-watch feature will undoubtedly contribute to fostering positive social interactions among users.
